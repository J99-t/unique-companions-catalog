Ferrets

Information

The ferret is a domesticated carnivorous mammal belonging to the same family as weasels and otters. Ferrets are famous for their high intelligence, curiosity, and their joyful hops. They are highly social and crepuscular creatures, meaning they are intensely active during dawn and dusk.

Environment

Ferrets are natural burrowers so they mainly would inhabit forest edges and grasslands. They are highly efficient hunters and this is reflected in their domesticated behavior when hunting toys or tunneling through any available crevice.

Comprehensive Care Guide

Habitat Requirements

  • Multi-Level Cage: A large, wire-mesh cage with solid floors to prevent paw injuries
  • Bedding: Soft blankets or hammocks. Avoid wood shavings or towels with loose loops that could catch their claws
  • Ferret-Proofing: Every hole, vent, and gap in their roaming area must be sealed to prevent ferrets from getting lost or stuck
  • Litter Boxes: Most ferrets can be litter trained but you should consider multiple boxes in their roaming areas as they have short digestive tracts
  • Temperature: Strict climate control is needed. They thrive best between 60°F - 75°F

Diet & Nutrition

Ferrets are carnivores with an extremely fast metabolism. They require a diet high in animal protein and fat but very low in fiber and carbohydrates. They cannot digest plant matter so it is suggested to feed them only high-quality ferret-specific kibble or raw whole-prey diets

Ownership Difficulty - Intermediate

Ferrets are classified as an intermediate difficulty due to their high medical needs and complexity of ferret-proofing a home
