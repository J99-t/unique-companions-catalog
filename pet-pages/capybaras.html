Capybaras

Information

Capybaras are the world's largest rodents and are found throughout the savannas and dense forests of South America. They are known to be docile and highly social and have become a viral sensation within the last couple of years. Despite their calm exterior, they are actually very complex creatures and require a lot of maintenance

Environment

Capybaras are always near water to hydrate their skin, regulate body temperature, and escape predators. Domestic environments must be able to replicate this semi-aquatic lifestyle which requires a secure large outdoor enclosure with a large temperature regulated pool.

Comprehensive Care Guide

Habitat Requirements

  • Water Access: A deep filtered pool or pond is mandatory since Capybaras must be able to fully submerge
  • Space & Fencing: Minimum 1,000 square feet of secure, non-climable fencing.
  • Climate: Highly sensitive to the cold. They require a insulated indoor space for winter months
  • Substrate: Grass/Pasture only. They are grazers and need to spend most of their day eating fresh grass
  • Bring a friend: Cabybaras are very social creatures so be prepared to adopt atleast one other capybara

Diet & Nutrition

Capybaras are graminivores, meaning that their diets consists entirely of grass and aquatic plants. In captivity they require high-quality orchard or Timothy hay. specialized supplement pellets and significant Vitamin C intake.

Ownership Difficulty - Advanced

Capybaras are classified in the advanced difficulty due to their special environmental and social needs
