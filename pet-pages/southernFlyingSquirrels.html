Southern Flying Squirrel

Information

The southern flying squirrel is a small nocturnal rodent. Despite their name, they don't actually fly instead utilize a specialized fold of skin to glide from tree to tree, steering with their flattened tails.

Environment

In their natural habitat, these squirrels nest in abandoned woodpecker holes or hollowed-out trees. They are rarely ever seen on the forest floor since they prefer to stay high in the canopy in order to avoid predators.

Comprehensive Care Guide

Habitat Requirements

  • Vertical Cage: A tall flight cage with a minimum height of 4-6 feet. Their cage bars shouldn't be larger than .5 inches as they can squeeze through.
  • Nesting Boxes: Multiple hanging nest boxes or soft pouches are essential so they can sleep in high secure locations
  • Climbing Structures: Manzanita branches, rope, and vertical ledges are needed to simulate their forest canopy home
  • Exercise wheel: A solid-surface stealth wheel is required to prevent their tail from getting caught in spokes
  • Social Needs: They are colony animals. It is highly recommended to keep them in pairs or small groups to prevent loneliness

Diet & Nutrition

Flying squirrels are omnivores. Their diets mainly consists of high-quality rodent blocks, a variety of nuts, seeds, fruits, and insects. They also have high requirement for calcium and Vitamin D3.

Ownership Difficulty - Advanced

Southern Flying Squirrels are considered advanced due to their nocturnal nature, social needs, and specialized environment
