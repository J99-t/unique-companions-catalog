Axolotls

Information

The Axolotl is a salamander thats capable of regrowing entire limbs, heart tissue, and even portions of its brain. Unlike other amphibians, they do not undergo metamorphosis to live on land.

Environment

Axolotls are natively exclusive to the freshwater lake complex of Xochimilco, Mexico, these water dogs thrive in cool, dark, high-altitude waters. Because they are critically endangered in the wild, the pet trade relies entirely on captive-bred populations.

Comprehensive Care Guide

Habitat Requirements

  • Tank size: Minimum of 20-29 gallons; long tanks are preferred over tall ones
  • Water Temperature: Must stay between 60°F - 68°F; heat is their #1 health risk
  • Substrate: Fine sand only (to prevent accidental ingestion and impaction)
  • Flow: Low-flow sponge filters are required to prevent gill stress
  • Lighting & Hides: No bright lighting and provide hides like caves or PVC pipes

Diet & Nutrition

Axolotls are carnivores that use a suction method to eat. Their diets primarily consists of nutrient-dense nightcrawlers, brine shrimp, or pellets

Ownership Difficulty - Intermediate

Axolotls are classified as an intermediate difficulty due to their specific environmental needs

🫳

Handling

Easy (1/10)

Strictly look but don't touch. Their permeable skin is highly sensitive to human oils and salts.
