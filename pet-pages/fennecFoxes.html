Fennec Foxes

Information

Fennec foxes are the smallest canine species in the world, famous for its massive ears and petite frame. Natively found in the Saharan Desert, their ears act as a natural cooling system to dissipate heat and allow the fox to hear prey moving underground.

Environment

Fennec foxes live in vast underground burrows. Because they are desert dwellers, they require consistent warmth and plenty of sand or soft substrate to satisfy their biological urge to dig. In a home setting, they cannot be caged like a hamster or left to roam like a cat. They require a dedicated escape-proof room or a massive outdoor enclosure with a dig-proof floor.

Comprehensive Care Guide

Habitat Requirements

  • Dedicated Space: A large walk-in enclosure or a fox-proofed room is required as they are agile jumpers and climbers
  • Digging Zone: You must provide a large sand pit or digging box or they will have a tendency to dig through carpet
  • Security: Double-entry doors are a must since foxes are very agile and can vanish through an open door in a split second
  • Temperature: Keep their environments between 70°F - 85°F
  • Sanitation: Fennec foxes can be difficult to litter train requiring a daily deep cleaning of their space

Diet & Nutrition

Fennecs are omnivores and their diets should primarily consists of a high-quality exotic canine kibble supplemented with raw meat, insects, and small amounts of fruits/vegetables.

Ownership Difficulty - Advanced

Fennec foxes are classified as an advanced difficulty due to their extreme energy, noise, and habitat security needs
